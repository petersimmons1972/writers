## Draft

I spent yesterday afternoon watching five AI agents try to build a website together.

Not watching code. Watching the engineers who manage them.

There's a guy named Martinez who sits in the corner with three monitors. His job is making sure the agents don't contradict each other. When the CSS agent decides the buttons should be blue and the accessibility agent says they need more contrast, Martinez is the one who notices. He types something. Waits. Types again.

You could see the exhaustion around 3 PM. Coffee cups everywhere. One engineer had her head down on the keyboard for maybe two minutes. Just resting.

The agents themselves—I don't know what they look like. Code scrolling past, I suppose. But Martinez and his team, they look like every other team I've seen when something's not working quite right and the deadline's tomorrow. Tired. Focused. That thing where someone rubs their eyes with both hands.

I asked Martinez what happens when two agents disagree. He said, "I become the tiebreaker. Every time. Sometimes fifty times a day."

The agents can write code faster than any human. That's not the interesting part. The interesting part is watching Martinez catch the moment when the database agent assumes MySQL and the deployment agent assumes PostgreSQL. Nobody told them to check with each other. They just... didn't.

Around 4 PM, something worked. One of the engineers actually said "Holy shit" out loud, then looked around like maybe he shouldn't have. The website loaded. All five agents had contributed something. It looked pretty good, I guess. I don't know much about websites.

But I know what it looked like when those six engineers stood up and stretched and started packing their laptops. Relief. The kind you see when something that could've gone wrong didn't.

We've got these AI agents now. They're fast. They're capable. They work all day without complaining.

But somebody still has to watch them. Somebody still has to make sure agent A knows what agent B decided. Somebody still has to be the tiebreaker fifty times a day.

That somebody drinks a lot of coffee and looks tired by 3 PM.
