# England Your England (1941)

**Source:** https://orwell.ru/library/essays/lion/english/e_eye
**Date:** January 19, 1941 (Part I of The Lion and the Unicorn)
**Genre:** Political Essay/Cultural Analysis
**Word Count:** ~10,000 words

## Summary

This essay, the first part of *The Lion and the Unicorn*, examines English national character during World War II. Written as German aircraft attack overhead, Orwell explores what makes England distinctive and worth defending.

## Key Themes

**National Identity and Patriotism**
Orwell argues that patriotism represents an overwhelming force that transcends class divisions. He contends that "the divisions between nation and nation are founded on real differences of outlook," suggesting each country possesses unique characteristics visible to any observer.

**English Distinctiveness**
The essay catalogs English traits: gentleness, love of flowers, devotion to hobbies, respect for law and constitutionalism, and resistance to militarism. Orwell notes the English paradoxically combine "hypocrisy and decency" within "a subtle network of compromises."

**Class Structure**
While acknowledging severe wealth inequality, Orwell observes that working and middle classes share deeper emotional unity than class antagonism. Most citizens "feel themselves to be a single nation" when confronted by external threats.

**Decline of the Ruling Class**
Orwell criticizes the decay of ability among England's governing elite, attributing their failures to willful ignorance about changing economic and political realities, particularly regarding fascism.

## Conclusion

Despite flaws, Orwell expresses faith in England's resilience and cultural continuity beyond the war.

- Critique of ruling class AND left intelligentsia
- Patriotism transcends ideology
- Long-form wartime analysis
